Methods to Check Your FESCO Bill Online

There are two simple and easy ways to check your fesco bill online. You can do so using either your 14-digit reference number or your 10-digit consumer ID, both of which can be found on your physical electricity bill.

1. Using the 14-Digit Reference Number

The 14-digit reference number is printed on the left side of your physical FESCO bill. To track your bill online, simply:

  1. Go to the FESCO e-bill tracking website.
  2. Enter your 14-digit reference number in the designated field.
  3. Click on the “Submit” button, and your bill details will appear on the screen.

2. Using the 10-Digit Consumer ID

The 10-digit consumer ID is located at the top left corner of your FESCO bill. If you prefer to use this ID to track your bill:

  1. Visit the same FESCO bill tracking portal.
  2. Input your 10-digit consumer ID into the appropriate field.
  3. After clicking “Submit,” you’ll instantly see the details of your current electricity bill.

Understanding FESCO and Its Role in Power Distribution

Faisalabad Electric Supply Company, or FESCO, was established in 1998 and has been serving its consumers for over two decades. FESCO is a subsidiary of the Water and Power Development Authority (WAPDA) and is responsible for distributing electricity to nearly 5.2 million consumers in the Faisalabad region and surrounding districts.

The company’s primary objective is to provide reliable electricity to both urban and rural areas, ensuring that even the most remote regions have access to consistent power.

Districts Served by FESCO

FESCO supplies electricity to eight districts in the central Punjab region of Pakistan. Each district has its own unique characteristics, but all share the same need for reliable electricity, which FESCO strives to provide.

  • Faisalabad: Known as the industrial hub of Pakistan, Faisalabad is famous for its thriving textile industry.
  • Jhang: This district is primarily recognized for its agriculture, producing crops such as wheat, sugarcane, and cotton.
  • Sargodha: Famous for its citrus orchards, particularly its kinnow, Sargodha is a vital part of Pakistan’s agricultural sector.
  • Toba Tek Singh: Rich in cultural heritage, Toba Tek Singh is a significant agricultural and historical region.
  • Chiniot: Known for its craftsmanship in furniture making, Chiniot is home to many skilled artisans.
  • Khushab: Rich in natural resources, Khushab is an important district for both agriculture and industry.
  • Mianwali: A district with beautiful landscapes, Mianwali is an important part of FESCO’s service area, with both urban and rural power needs.
  • Bhakkar: Another district served by FESCO, Bhakkar is known for its agricultural activities and rural landscapes.
